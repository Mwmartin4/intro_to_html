Chattanooga is a diverse, beautiful place. The population on the human side isn't any different, with some interesting facts about them and their differences highlighted here.

Chattanooga

  • According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the racial makeup of Tennessee in 2020 was 74.3% White, 16.3% Black or African American, 2.7% Asian, 1.1% American Indian or Alaska Native, and 0.2% Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander.
  • The largest city in Tennessee, Memphis, has a majority Black population, with African Americans comprising over 63% of the city's residents..
  • The city of Nashville, the capital of Tennessee, has a more diverse population, with White residents comprising around 56% of the population, Black residents comprising around 28%, and Hispanic/Latinx residents comprising around 10%.
  • Tennessee has a long history of racial tension and segregation, particularly during the Jim Crow era. The state was a center of the Civil Rights Movement, with the famous lunch counter sit-ins in Nashville in 1960 and the assassination of Martin Luther King Jr. in Memphis in 1968.
    Today, Tennessee is home to a growing number of immigrants and refugees, particularly from Latin America, Asia, and Africa. In recent years, the state has seen an increase in the number of Hispanic/Latinx residents, who now comprise over 5% of the state's population.
